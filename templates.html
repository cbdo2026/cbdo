XXIII Brazilian Colloquium on Orbital Dynamics

Abstract Template & Guidelines

Abstracts for the XXIII CBDO must be prepared exclusively using the official LaTeX template.

Download the LaTeX abstract template

Abstract submission is part of the registration form. Participants must upload both the compiled PDF file and the corresponding .tex source file.

Abstract Submission Guidelines

  • Submission deadline: 31 July 2026, 23:59.
  • Number of submissions: Each participant may submit up to two contributions as presenting author.
  • Presenting author: The name of the presenting author must be indicated in bold in the abstract.
  • Length: The abstract must not exceed one page, including any additional information such as references, acknowledgements, or funding information.
  • Figures and tables: Abstracts must not contain figures or tables.
  • Required files: A compiled PDF version and the corresponding .tex file must be submitted through the registration form.
  • File names: Files must be clearly identified with the name of the presenting author, for example:
    Full_Name_01.tex, Full_Name_01.pdf, Full_Name_02.tex, Full_Name_02.pdf.

Use of Artificial Intelligence

In accordance with the CNPq Policy on Integrity in Scientific Activity, the use of Artificial Intelligence at any stage of the development of the work submitted to the XXIII CBDO must be explicitly declared in the corresponding abstract.

The declaration must indicate the name of the AI tool and the type of use, such as text revision, slide preparation, figure or table preparation, code generation, data analysis, or any other relevant use.

Poster Guidelines

  • Format: Posters must be prepared in A0 format.
  • Mounting: Posters must have support bars at the top and bottom, with a string for hanging on a tripod-style stand.
  • Important: It will not be possible to attach posters to walls or flat surfaces.
